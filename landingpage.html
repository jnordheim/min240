Skate Appreciate

Skate Appreciate is a site where skaters can search for the closest park so they can land their next trick.

The maps feature of the app allows skaters to find parks in their vicinity. Similar to a GPS, it will tell you what parks are buzzing and which ones are vacant. Depending on the person, they can choose to go to a park of their choosing.
Finding people to skate with is harder than it seems. Not having a skate partner is like playing tennis by yourself; nobody is there to push you. That's why having the linking up feature is essential for getting better as a skater.
At the end of the day, the goal of a skater is to record your tricks and upload them (or get sponsored, if you're trying to go pro). The tricks timeline of the app allows skaters watch their peers' tricks and to commend them for their hardwork. And of course show off their own tricks!

Design Process

An Interview with Walker Johnson: fellow local skater

Background: Walker is a sophomore student at Northern Kentucky University.

How long have you been skating for?

For about four years total.

What’s been the biggest obstacle for you in skating?

Getting hurt, mostly. I am very weak and have brittle bones. It doesn't take a lot to be out for a while.

Where do you see Northern Kentucky skate scene in five years?

I’ve just moved up here so I don't know really, but ollie’s is closing. I only was able to go one time and it was dead. However, I don't think it was a lack of people skating, I think it's a lack of people knowing about the place.

What do you think about a social media for people where they can rate skate parks, upload tricks, and meet other skaters?

I think it would bring a people together, which is never a bad thing. It brings awareness of places other than campus for people to learn at.

Insight

  1. Theme: Awareness
    • Knowledge of local skateparks.
    • The ability of rating these skateparks.
  2. Theme: Community
    • Bringing a small community together.
    • Encouraging friends based on their uploads.
